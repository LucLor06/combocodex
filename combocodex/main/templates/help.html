General

What is a team combo?

A team combo is simply a true combo that involves two or more players that kills starting at zero damage. Some may argue a team combo does not need to kill starting from zero, but that falls more under the term of a "followup" than a full team combo.

Notating team combos

While there are many different ways people have come up to notate team combos, the most simple is generally using ">" to indicate a weapon switch and stating the weapon/legend that starts the combo.

A Kaya (Spear) Asuri (Sword) combo would be notated as follows:

Sword nlight > nlight > dlight sair > dsig > dsig > dlight sair > gc nsig

It is common to not include movement within the combo notation, on the other hand gravity cancels are commonly notated.

Terminology

True

There are no dodge frames (the opponent cannot dodge) between two or more consecutive moves.

Directional Influence (DI)

When two of the same moves on the same weapon hit in a true combo, the opponent can use directional influence to move out of the combo despite it being theoretically inescapable. Different variations of moves (e.g. slight fused/split on Chakram or slight bridge/start on Greatsword) still count towards DI. For more detailed information watch this great video by Swirft.
